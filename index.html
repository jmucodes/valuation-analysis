Market Analysis

Dominican Republic iGaming — Ground Floor of a Regulated Market

The DR formally regulated online gambling in 2024 under Resolution 136-2024. Sports betting licenses cost $260K, online casino licenses $346K, and they're valid for 5 years. The licensing process averages ~2 years, creating a significant barrier to entry and first-mover advantage for holders. Appuesta is pursuing the sports betting license first, with online casino as a future expansion phase.

Key Insight: With only 4 active licensees and a 2-year application process, Appuesta's near-final license status is itself a scarce, defensible asset. A new competitor entering today would not be operational until 2028.

Deal Structure

Recommended Instrument & Terms

Option A: SAFE Note

Simple Agreement for Future Equity (SAFE) — invented by Y Combinator, now the standard instrument for early-stage raises. Here's how it works in plain terms:

The investor gives you money now in exchange for the right to receive equity later — specifically when a "trigger event" happens (usually your next priced funding round, or an acquisition). The investor does NOT get shares immediately. Instead, their investment converts into shares at that future event, at a price determined by the valuation cap you agree to today.

Example: Investor puts in $100K on a SAFE with a $4M cap. When you later raise a Series A at $15M valuation, their $100K converts at the $4M cap — meaning they get shares as if the company was worth $4M, not $15M. This rewards them for investing early.

Key advantages: No interest payments, no maturity/repayment date, no board seats required, minimal legal costs (~$5K vs. $25K+ for a priced round), and it's a single document — fast to execute.

  • Valuation cap: $4M-$5M
  • No discount (or 10% max)
  • Pro-rata rights for investor (right to maintain % in future rounds)
  • Most founder-friendly option — no dilution until conversion
RECOMMENDED
Option B: Priced Equity Round

Direct equity sale. More complex but gives investor immediate ownership stake and clear terms.

  • Pre-money: $4M
  • Board observer seat (no vote)
  • Quarterly reporting requirement
  • Preferred shares with 1x liquidation preference
ALTERNATIVE

For a raise of this size ($500K-$750K) from likely angel investors or strategic partners in DR, a SAFE note is simpler and faster. If raising from an institutional VC or a single large investor, a priced round may be preferred.

  • Exit Paths: Acquisition by larger LATAM operator (Betsson, Codere, Rush Street), or scale to Series A once DR revenue data proves the model.

Action Items

Questions to Answer Before Investor Meetings

The following data points, once gathered, will significantly strengthen the valuation case and fill gaps that a sophisticated investor will ask about. Discuss these at your next co-founder meeting:

Financial & Unit Economics
  • What is our actual GGR and NGR by month since platform launch (July 2025)? Break out sportsbook vs. casino if possible. This lets us calculate true hold % and margin.
  • What are our actual bonus costs by month? We know GGR but not net bonus costs — this is the single biggest gap in our financial story.
  • What is our blended CAC to date? Total marketing spend ÷ total depositors (500+). Even a rough number helps.
  • What is average deposit size and frequency for our top 20% of players? This gives us LTV projections an investor can trust.
  • What does our monthly P&L actually look like? Even rough — revenue, provider costs (Betby/Flexsy), payment fees, marketing, salaries, G&A. Are we contribution-margin positive on existing players?
DR Market Specifics
  • Who are the 4 current DR licensees? Names, what they offer, how established they are. Knowing the competitive landscape lets us position against them directly.
  • What is the Escogido deal worth in dollar terms? Get a quote for what comparable stadium signage/sponsorship costs. Even an informal ask to the team's commercial department gives us a defensible number.
  • What is the billboard provider's rate card? Get the actual per-billboard monthly rate so we can quantify the in-kind value precisely (currently estimated at $51K-$102K).
  • What sports are most popular to bet on in DR? Baseball (winter league + MLB) is obvious, but what about basketball, boxing, football? This informs our Betby content strategy for DR launch.
  • What's the competitive landscape for payment acceptance? Can the other 4 operators accept local bank transfers and credit cards, or are they offshore-only? If we're the only ones with CARDNET, that's a massive talking point.
Strategic & Legal
  • Exact license timeline and remaining steps. Any risk of delay beyond 60 days? What's the absolute worst case?
  • What entity will hold the DR license? DRBETS SRL? This matters for how the investment is structured (which entity the SAFE attaches to).
  • What's the plan for the online casino license? Timeline, estimated cost, and when to apply. Investors will want to know this is on the roadmap even if not in this raise.
  • Do any of the three founders have non-compete or conflict-of-interest issues? An investor will ask — better to surface and address now.
  • Cap table clarity. What is the exact ownership split among the three founders today? Any prior investors, advisors, or option holders?
Valuation Boosters — Data That Could Push Valuation Higher
  • If you can show positive unit economics (LTV > CAC, even on small sample), the valuation conversation shifts from $4M toward $5-6M.
  • If you can get a signed LOI from Escogido (even a simple one-page letter of intent), it converts a handshake into a tangible asset.
  • If you can confirm the other 4 operators DON'T have local bank accounts / credit card acceptance, that's a defensible competitive moat worth highlighting.
  • If you can get the billboard deal committed in writing (even an email confirmation), it becomes a valued in-kind contribution to the pitch.
  • If you have any user testimonials or engagement data from Chile/Ecuador — even anecdotal ("our top player bets X times per week") — it humanizes the numbers.
  • If you can quantify the DR market more precisely — the $370 ARPU and 16% penetration from the pamphlet are good, but sourcing from an industry report (H2 Gambling Capital, Statista, etc.) makes it audit-proof for investors.
